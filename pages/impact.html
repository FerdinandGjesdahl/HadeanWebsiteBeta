The Hadean Impact

Hadean Ventures has a strong commitment to being a driver for positive changes in society as a whole.

Responsible investing = Value creation

The firm contributes to increased wealth, better health and improved living conditions across the globe through support of research and development and with a strong focus on environmental, social and governance (ESG) matters.

Hadean Ventures welcomes the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR), which links the UN's 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development and the Paris Agreement on climate change to the due-diligence processes and investment-making decisions of financial institutions. These regulations make finance a powerful tool for creating overall value.

The investment due-diligence process has been tailored to meet high ESG standards and technical standards will continue to be implemented. Through impactful positions such as board directorships, Hadean Ventures uses its influence to implement ESG policies and standard operating procedures in portfolio companies.

Strong focus on diversity and gender equality

The firm's ESG policy guides its consideration of environmental, social and governance factors within both the investment firm itself and the companies it invests in. Lack of diversity and gender equality is identified as a major challenge in the investment industry and start-up community. Hadean Ventures works for an inclusive and diverse environment within its firm and portfolio companies and promotes diversity and equal opportunity in recruitment processes.

Programs are in place to help increase diversity both in start-ups and in investment roles. Hadean Ventures participates in cross-firm, cross-border initiatives such as Level20, which it helped launch in the Nordics in 2019 together with several other private-equity firms.

Sustainability goals

The UN Sustainable Development Goals directly impacted by the firm include good health and well-being, gender equality, industry and innovation, and responsible consumption and production. The launch of the SDGs in 2015 underscored the role of the private sector in solving global challenges. Following the launch of the SFDR in the EU/EEA, financial market participants are encouraged to contribute to sustainable investments through their business activities, asset allocation and investment decisions (UNPRI).
